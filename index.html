About Me

Hi, I’m Brenda and I am interested in building meaningful applications or games for society.

I enjoy the process of design as we would never know where we would get to but we would go through iterations of iterations before finally being relatively satisfied with a design. However, design does not stop there. There is always room for improvement and the best way to improve is to listen to the users’ feedback.

I aspire to create games that can make an impact on our lives; Games with meaningful stories and characters that may be able to inculcate certain values in the players or increase their awareness about a certain issue; Games which allow us to connect with our family, friends and even to make new friends around the world.

I hope that games can not only allow its players to relax and bring joy to them, but also allow players to learn something new :)

Perdition

Perdition is a top-down shooter PC game made using Unity game engine. I was mainly in charge of the UI/UX, story and the implementation of some game features.

You may view more details in the documentation links below.

Void Rooms

Void Rooms is a virtual reality (VR) escape room game made using Unity game engine. The player has to explore the rooms, interact with objects and solve riddles to find the way out.

I was in charge of the audio design and creating clue assets which contain background story of the location that the player is currently in.

Jill and the Beanstalk

The objective of the game is to protect the growing beanstalk from waves of enemies. This was made using Unity game engine during a 40-hour game jam for the Game Development and Design Exam.

I had a storyline idea before creating this game. The story is about Jill looking for her brother who has went missing for months. The only place left to search is the world above the clouds. Jill attempts to grow a magic beanstalk but its magic essence seemed to have attracted some curious creatures. And so, we are on a mission to protect the beanstalk until it grows beyond the clouds.

Episode Interactive

Episode is an app which provides a platform for users to write and direct their stories by coding.

I wrote a story called 'Ancestors' which is mainly about a village girl entering Town to find the owner of a book. The story takes place in ancient China so I researched its history for a more realistic story.

After planning the character background and script, I used the Episode platform to design my characters, and used code to direct the scenes in the story.

Mr Love: Producers' Secrets

I met some South East Asian friends through Facebook, with a common interest - a romance simulation game known as 'Mr Love: Dream Date'. We decided to create a fanpage to post memes and fan creations.

I designed our fanpage profile picture, logo, and cover photo. Currently, I am the only page admin managing the fanpage. The fan videos and scripts are mostly made by me.

Mush!

Mush! is a text-based role-playing game made using Python programming language. Players explore the room for items/buffs before encountering a mushroom enemy.

I implemented the story and exploration portion of the game while my partner made the battle part.

Courage . Purpose . Character

This is a stop motion video made in 2017, which won a prize in a school event. The given theme was the school values - Courage, Purpose and Character.

I planned the story with 3 characters representing each value. The trio was stranded on an island. Although there were some obstacles, they worked together and eventually returned home.

P.S. The video is in Chinese and its speed is fast due to the given time limit. I have added English subtitles so turn on the captions if you wish to.

CoFe Toilet

This is a school design project about improving the layout of toilets for the pandemic. The problem we focused to solve was social distancing in toilets. We researched and studied the toilet designs in our surroundings in order to understand how toilets are designed.

Although it was tedious to create numerous iterations of our toilet layout design, I enjoyed the design process. The pros and cons for each layout change with every iteration and we have to "experiment" to obtain the best solution. You may watch a video which summarises our project or visit the website to find out more.

P.S. Project Part 4 in the website summarises our final product.
